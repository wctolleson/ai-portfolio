Togal.AI Estimating Evaluation

AI-Powered Construction Takeoff Analysis Across Multiple Building Types

Project Overview

This project evaluates Togal.AI, an AI-powered construction takeoff and estimating tool, by testing its capabilities across multiple building types and drawing formats. The goal was to assess the tool's accuracy, identify its strengths and limitations, and document the manual corrections required to produce reliable quantity data.

Buildings Evaluated

  • Multi-Story Hotel
  • Youth Center
  • Fire Station (2 floors)
  • Building Elevation
  • Military Office (2 floors)

Building 1: Multi-Story Hotel

Togal.AI Raw Output Issues

  • Canopy with foundations not shown — AI did not identify structural canopy elements
  • Pool not filled in — Water features not properly recognized
  • Hot tub not identified — Smaller amenity features missed
  • Patio and porches not identified — Outdoor living spaces excluded from takeoff

Corrections Applied

Manual markup required to identify exterior amenity areas and structural features that the AI classification system missed.

Building 2: Youth Center

Togal.AI Raw Output Issues

  • Foundation plan not recognized — AI did not identify the drawing as a foundation plan
  • Grade beams not identified — Critical structural elements missed entirely
  • Column bay outside building envelope — Incorrectly included area that needed manual removal

Corrected Data

  • Footprint: 18,647.67 SF
  • Net Area: 29,146.75 SF

Building 3: Fire Station (1st & 2nd Floor)

Overall Assessment

✓ Impressive Performance: Overall, Togal was very impressive on this fire station floorplan. Polygons were not too far off from the start.

Items Added Manually

Found myself looking for fixtures and equipment that weren't marked:

  • Fire poles
  • Water fountain
  • Dishwasher

2nd Floor Issue

  • "Open to below" not recognized — Togal appears to think there is a slab where there is actually a double-height space

Corrected Data

First Floor:

  • Footprint: 6,753.16 SF
  • Net Area: 6,042.51 SF
  • Doors Area: 44.34 SF

Building 4: Building Elevation

Togal.AI Raw Output Issues

⚠ Significant Issues: The Togal.AI first pass on the elevation did not pick up the majority of the building.

Problems Encountered

  • Hatching recognition required — Had to use tools to identify concrete panels, brick veneer, and louvers
  • Column lines filled incorrectly — Column lines were being filled in as part of the building
  • Glass pattern recognition failed — Did not work on this elevation

New Classifications Added

  • Brick veneer
  • Louvers
  • Other façade elements

Building 5: Military Office (1st & 2nd Floor)

Togal.AI Raw Output Issues

  • Open-space utility room misidentified — Togal thinks this area is inside the building when it's not
  • Concrete slabs not recognized — Structural elements missed
  • Ceiling types not distinguished — No differentiation between grid ceiling, gypsum, and open air no-ceiling areas
  • Polygons not separated — Drop-in grid ceiling and metal soffit system combined incorrectly

Corrected Data

  • Footprint: 1,779.63 SF (734.07 SF + 1,045.56 SF)
  • Net Area: 1,097.12 SF

Key Findings

Strengths

  • Strong performance on standard floor plans
  • Good polygon accuracy for common room types
  • Effective for rapid first-pass takeoffs
  • Time savings on routine spaces

Limitations

  • Struggles with foundation plans
  • Elevation takeoffs need significant manual work
  • Misses specialty fixtures and equipment
  • Doesn't recognize "open to below" conditions
  • Ceiling type differentiation needs improvement
  • Glass pattern recognition unreliable

Conclusions

Togal.AI is a valuable tool for accelerating the takeoff process, particularly for floor plans with standard room configurations. However, it requires professional oversight and manual correction to produce reliable estimates.

Best practices for using Togal.AI:

  • Always review raw output against original drawings
  • Plan for manual additions of specialty items (fixtures, equipment)
  • Expect significant correction work on elevations and foundations
  • Verify building envelope boundaries
  • Check multi-story conditions (open to below, double-height spaces)
